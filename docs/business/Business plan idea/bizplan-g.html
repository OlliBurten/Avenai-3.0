Solacia

Simplifying Post-Loss Administration with Empathy and Automation.

Solacia is an online platform designed to alleviate the significant administrative burden on individuals following the death of a family member or legal partner. We replace a fragmented, emotionally draining, and bureaucratic process with a centralized, supportive, and efficient digital solution.

The Core Problem

Settling an estate is overwhelming. Survivors face a complex web of tasks, from contacting banks and insurers to managing government agencies, all while grieving. There is no existing holistic platform to guide them through this journey. We are a first-mover platform within a large, underserved market.

The Problem in Numbers

  • 100+ Hours Lost: The average family spends over 100 hours on administrative tasks alone after a loss.3
  • €2,300+ in Avoidable Fees: Many routine administrative tasks are unnecessarily outsourced to lawyers, leading to significant costs.5
  • Dozens of Digital Accounts: The average person leaves behind 25-30 online accounts, creating a complex digital estate to manage.4

Initial Target Market

The European digital legacy market is currently valued at approximately €4.5 billion and is projected to reach €7.5 billion by 2030. This growth is driven by Europe's aging population and the increasing need for digital solutions to manage post-loss administration.1,2

Phase 1 Markets: Sweden & Norway

These countries are ideal first markets due to their high digital maturity. Sweden has an estimated 92,000 deaths per year, while Norway has approximately 45,000, representing a significant initial serviceable market. The total addressable market (TAM) for digital legacy administration is an estimated €200M per year across these two countries.9,10

Phase 2 Markets: Denmark, Finland, Austria & Italy

Our second phase targets countries with high digital readiness. Denmark has an estimated 59,000 deaths per year, and Finland has ~57,000 deaths per year. The combined annual addressable market for these phase two countries is approximately €450M.11,12

Our Solution: A Dual-Channel Platform

Our comprehensive product suite serves individual consumers directly (B2C) while also providing a powerful tool for institutional partners (B2B). This dual approach creates a robust ecosystem, capturing value from multiple points in the estate settlement process.

Appendix A: Market & Competitive Analysis

The Demographic Imperative: Europe's Great Wealth Transfer

The opportunity for Solacia is fundamentally driven by a massive, once-in-a-generation demographic shift. Europe's population is aging, a direct result of the high fertility rates during the "baby boom" of the 1950s and 1960s. As these Baby Boomers retire and age, they are set to transfer an unprecedented amount of wealth to their heirs, primarily Generation X. This "Great Wealth Transfer" creates an urgent and growing need for services that can simplify the complex process of estate administration.8

Competitive Landscape

The European market for digital bereavement services is nascent and highly fragmented. There is currently no single, dominant player offering a holistic, pan-European solution. The competition can be categorized as follows:

Indirect Competitors

Traditional law firms, accountants, and the internal, often manual, bereavement departments within banks and insurance companies.

Partial Digital Competitors

Startups offering point solutions like digital estate planning tools, password managers, and online will-creation services.

Adjacent Support Services

Organizations like the Bereavement Network Europe and Cruse Bereavement Support (UK) who provide emotional support but not administrative tools.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • First-mover advantage in a fragmented market
  • Holistic platform (not a point solution)
  • Dual B2C/B2B model creating a strong moat

Weaknesses

  • B2C usage is finite by nature
  • Requires building a new, high-trust brand

Opportunities

  • The "Great Wealth Transfer" from Baby Boomers
  • Government partnerships for digital identity (EUDI)
  • Expansion into adjacent financial planning services

Threats

  • Cybersecurity and data privacy regulations
  • Large financial institutions building in-house solutions
  • Slow cultural adoption of digital solutions

Key Challenges & Mitigation Strategies

Challenge: Justifying Aggressive Financial Projections

The forecast's rapid scaling may be met with skepticism from investors accustomed to longer growth cycles for enterprise SaaS.

Mitigation Strategy:

Our model's feasibility is grounded in a capital-efficient, high-margin SaaS model and a unique B2C-to-B2B leverage strategy.

  • De-Risking Adoption: Pilot programs in Years 1-2 will validate the product and build powerful case studies to accelerate sales cycles.
  • B2C as Leverage: The B2C channel acts as a strategic customer acquisition funnel, providing a large user base that acts as leverage in B2B negotiations.
  • High-Margin Model: A lean, AI-augmented operational model ensures costs scale slower than the user base, allowing profits to accelerate quickly.

Challenge: Potentially High Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

Acquiring customers for a sensitive, high-trust service can be expensive (Fintech CAC > €900).

Mitigation Strategy:

  • Partnerships as Primary Channel: Our B2B model is our core customer acquisition strategy, creating a "warm lead" funnel that bypasses the high costs of cold, top-of-funnel marketing.
  • Trust-Based Content Marketing: Our digital marketing will focus on high-quality content that establishes our expertise (E-E-A-T), attracting organic traffic at a lower cost.

Challenge: Long-Term Defensibility & Competition

A first-mover advantage is temporary; a sustainable competitive moat is required.

Mitigation Strategy:

  • The B2B Partnership Moat: Our primary defense is our network of sticky, long-term B2B contracts with high switching costs.
  • The Empathy & UX Moat: Our human-first, compassionate UX builds a level of brand trust that is difficult for a purely tech-focused competitor to replicate.
  • The Regulatory & Operational Moat: Our strategic alignment with the EUDI wallet framework and deep expertise in European legal frameworks create an intellectual property moat that is not easily copied.

Challenge: Securing a Venture-Scale "Home Run" Return

Our base case 20x return is strong, but top-tier VCs often look for 50-100x opportunities.

Mitigation Strategy:

The 20x projection is the floor, not the ceiling. The path to a venture-scale return is clear:

  • Accelerated Exit: Our business is an ideal acquisition target for a global financial institution in Years 5-7, which would significantly boost the annualized return (IRR).
  • Geographic Expansion: A strategic investment could accelerate our entry into the multi-billion dollar North American market, creating a massive new growth curve not reflected in current projections.

Speaker's Notes: Pitch & Partnership Strategy

For internal use only. High-level talking points for framing conversations with investors and potential institutional partners.

On Partnering with a Pilot Institution

When discussing an equity stake with a potential pilot partner, it is crucial to frame the conversation strategically to ensure a mutually beneficial long-term relationship.

Acknowledge Potential Drawbacks

Proactively address concerns around conflicts of interest or becoming a "captive" vendor. This builds trust.

Emphasize the Strategic Upside

Focus on the immense benefits: market validation, a guaranteed first customer to de-risk adoption, and strategic access to their network and expertise.

Pitching the Investment to a Partner

Our approach should be a three-step process focused on mutual benefit and strategic foresight.

  • Frame it as a Partnership, not a Transaction: Emphasize that their brand and industry expertise are as valuable as their capital. The equity stake is about creating a deeper, synergistic relationship.
  • Proactively Address Conflicts: Propose a governance structure that ensures our neutrality, such as a non-exclusive agreement and an Advisory Board seat rather than a full Board seat.
  • Present a Clear ROI: Focus on how Solacia will help them reduce operational costs, improve customer retention, and gain a significant competitive edge. This is an investment in their own future efficiency and profitability.
